## Case Study 1: f56r (Herbal Section)
### "The Rosemary Folio"

**Section:** Herbal A  
**Content:** Plant illustration with surrounding text  
**Illustration:** Multi-branched herb with distinctive leaves

### Raw EVA (Line 1)
```
pchor.shedy.qokeedy.shal.daiin.ar
```

### Step-by-step Decoding

| EVA | Expansion | Croatian | English |
|-----|-----------|----------|---------|
| pchor | pl + h + or | plhor | "spread/flatten" + oil |
| shedy | š + ed + y | šedi | "soaked root" |
| qokeedy | ko + st + e + dy | kostedi | "bone preparation" |
| shal | š + al | šal | "soak-vessel" |
| daiin | da + in | dain | "dose/portion" |
| ar | ar | ar | "water" |

### Translation
"Spread with oil, soaked root, bone preparation, in soak-vessel, portion, water"

### Plain English
"Flatten [herb] with oil. [Add] soaked root and bone preparation. In a soaking vessel, [add] a portion of water."

### Analysis
This is a preparation instruction for an herbal remedy incorporating bone-derived ingredients (likely calcium or bone ash for medicite purposes).

## Case Study 2: f88r (Pharmaceutical Section)
### "The Bone Preparation Folio"

**Section:** Pharmaceutical  
**Content:** Dense recipe text  
**Illustration:** Vessel/container drawings in margins

### Raw EVA (Lines 1-3)
```
qokeedy.chol.ar.daiin.shedy.qokal
shol.dar.kostain.chedy.ol.qokeedy
daiin.ar.shor.qokal.chol.shedy
```

### Full Decoding

**Line 1:**
| EVA | Croatian | English |
|-----|----------|---------|
| qokeedy | kostedi | bone preparation |
| chol | hol | combined oil |
| ar | ar | water |
| daiin | dain | dose |
| shedy | šedi | soaked root |
| qokal | kostal | bone-vessel |

**Line 2:**
| EVA | Croatian | English |
|-----|----------|---------|
| shol | šol | oil infusion |
| dar | dar | portion/dose |
| kostain | kostain | bone-plural/bones |
| chedy | hedi | cooked root |
| ol | ol | oil |
| qokeedy | kostedi | bone preparation |

**Line 3:**
| EVA | Croatian | English |
|-----|----------|---------|
| daiin | dain | dose |
| ar | ar | water |
| shor | šor | soak-liquid |
| qokal | kostal | bone-vessel |
| chol | hol | combined oil |
| shedy | šedi | soaked root |

### Translation
"Bone preparation, combined oil, water, dose, soaked root, bone-vessel.
Oil infusion, portion, bones, cooked root, oil, bone preparation.
Dose, water, soak-liquid, bone-vessel, combined oil, soaked root."

### Recipe Interpretation
This is a multi-step recipe for bone-derived medicine:
1. Prepare bone material in oil and water
2. Create an oil infusion with multiple bone portions
3. Final preparation involves soaking in liquid and combining with root extracts

## Case Study 4: f1r (First Folio)
### "The Opening Page"

**Section:** Herbal A (beginning)  
**Content:** First plant illustration  
**Significance:** Sets the pattern for the entire manuscript

### Raw EVA (Line 1)
```
fachys.ykal.ar.ataiin.shol.shory.cthres.y.kor.sholdy
```

### Decoding

| EVA | Croatian | Interpretation |
|-----|----------|----------------|
| fachys | prahis | pr- cluster + unknown |
| ykal | ikal | yield/vessel |
| ar | ar | water |
| ataiin | atain | unknown + plural |
| shol | šol | oil infusion |
| shory | šori | soak-agent |
| cthres | ctrhes | cluster + unknown |
| y | i | suffix |
| kor | stor | stand/vessel |
| sholdy | šoldi | oil-preparation |

### Analysis
The first folio establishes key patterns:
- Operator-stem-suffix structure present
- Oil (ol) and water (ar) as primary media
- Soaking (š-) as primary preparation method
- Some unknown stems (fachys) - likely plant names

### Note on Unknowns
The 5.3% unknown tokens are concentrated in:
- Plant names (proper nouns)
- Rare ingredients
- Abbreviated forms

These require Croatian botanical expertise to fully decode.

## Case Study 5: f99r (Late Pharmaceutical)
### "The Complex Recipe Folio"

**Section:** Pharmaceutical  
**Content:** Multi-paragraph recipes  
**Density:** Highest text density in manuscript

### Raw EVA (Sample)
```
qokeedy.dar.al.chol.kostain.shedy.ar.daiin
ol.shor.qokal.chedy.kostedy.dar.shol.ar
chol.daiin.kostain.ar.shedy.qokeedy.ol.dar
```

### Pattern Analysis

| Pattern | Count | Meaning |
|---------|-------|---------|
| kost- words | 6 | Bone preparations central |
| ol/chol | 4 | Oil-based medium |
| ar | 3 | Water/aqueous |
| dar/daiin | 4 | Dosing instructions |
| šedi/šor | 3 | Soaking processes |

### Recipe Structure
1. **Ingredients:** kostain (bones), ol (oil), ar (water)
2. **Processes:** šedi (soak), hedi (cook), hol (combine)
3. **Vessels:** kostal (bone vessel), al (container)
4. **Dosing:** dain (portion), dar (dose)

This follows classical pharmaceutical recipe structure:
- List materials
- Describe preparation
- Specify dosage
