# Croatian Botanical Glossary for ZFD
## Extracted from Ljekaruše Tradition + Šulek (1879) + Dürrigl/Fatović-Ferenčić Research

**Purpose:** Map Latin pharmaceutical terms to medieval Croatian vernacular (Chakavian/Glagolitic tradition) for Voynich herbal section enrichment (folios 1-66).

**Sources:**
1. Šulek, *Jugoslavenski imenik bilja* (1879) - Internet Archive full text OCR
2. Dürrigl & Fatović-Ferenčić, Žgombić Miscellany recipes (16th c., reflecting older tradition)
3. Dioscorides recipes in Croatian ljekaruše (Krnjak 2019, overview paper 2022)
4. Recipes Project blog post with transcribed Glagolitic toothache recipes

### Key: Multiple folk names were recorded for each plant. Šulek drew from:
- **ML** = Miklosich Latin

- **Sblj** = Šulek's own compilation
- **Brek** = Breko collection
- **Vis** = Visian/island dialect
- **Dan** = Daniel collection
- **Stulli** = Stulli dictionary (Dubrovnik)

## ARCHITECTURE NOTE (Per Curio's Analysis)

The "glossary" we need is not a standalone dictionary. It is assembled from:

1. **Šulek 1879** - aggregated folk names (available, 582 pages, Internet Archive OCR)
2. **Glagolitic recipe transcriptions** - scattered across academic papers
3. **Krnjak 2019 dissertation** - comprehensive analysis (PDF access blocked, need alternate route)
4. **Dürrigl & Fatović-Ferenčić papers** - specific plant identifications from manuscripts
5. **Staroslavenski institut dictionary** - Church Slavonic botanical terms

### Priority Manuscripts (per Curio):
- **Milčetić 1913 transcription** of oldest (late 14th c.) Croatian Glagolitic ljekaruša at HAZU
- **Žgombić Miscellany** (early 16th c.) - medical sections transcribed
- **Strohal's collection** (= "Razni zapisi i čaranja", early 15th c.)
- **Karlobaške ljekaruše** (1603, 1707) - published by HAZU with Fatović-Ferenčić editing
- **Vrbnik (Island of Krk)** fragments - any medical text from here is high-priority
