## Primary Sources

### The Voynich Manuscript

**Beinecke MS 408**. General Collection, Beinecke Rare Book and Manuscript Library, Yale University.
- Digital facsimile: https://collections.library.yale.edu/catalog/2002046
- High-resolution scans freely available

**Transcriptions:**

Landini, G., & Zandbergen, R. (1998). *Interlinear Voynich Manuscript Transcription (IVTFF)*. EVA encoding standard.
- Source for word frequency analysis
- Used in ZFD validation pipeline

Takahashi, T. (1998). *Complete EVA transcription of the Voynich Manuscript*.
- First complete character-by-character transcription

## Glagolitic Script Sources

### Manuscripts Referenced

**Hrvoje's Missal** (Missale Hervoiae), c. 1404.
- Croatian Academy of Sciences and Arts, Zagreb
- Contemporary with Voynich creation period
- Formal angular Glagolitic bookhand

**Vinodolski Zakon** (Vinodol Codex), 1288 (15th c. copies).
- Cursive Glagolitic legal document
- Key comparison for scribal behavior

**Misal kneza Novaka** (Missal of Prince Novak), 1368.
- Earlier liturgical comparison

**Petrisov Zbornik** (Petris's Collection), 15th c.
- Mixed hand examples

### Glagolitic Studies

Žagar, M. (2013). *Grafolingvistika srednjovjekovnih tekstova*. Zagreb: Matica hrvatska.
- Croatian medieval text linguistics

Hercigonja, E. (1994). *Tropismena i trojezična kultura hrvatskoga srednjovjekovlja*. Zagreb: Matica hrvatska.
- Three-script, trilingual Croatian medieval culture

Štefanić, V. (1969). *Glagoljski rukopisi otoka Krka*. Zagreb: JAZU.
- Glagolitic manuscripts of Krk island

Damjanović, S. (2004). *Slovo iskona*. Zagreb: Matica hrvatska.
- Introduction to Croatian Glagolitic tradition

Fučić, B. (1982). *Glagoljski natpisi*. Zagreb: JAZU.
- Glagolitic inscriptions catalog

## Voynich Manuscript Studies

### Key Academic Works

D'Imperio, M. E. (1978). *The Voynich Manuscript: An Elegant Enigma*. National Security Agency.
- Foundational survey of research to 1978
- NSA publication, declassified

Kennedy, G., & Churchill, R. (2006). *The Voynich Manuscript*. London: Orion Books.
- Comprehensive popular overview

Clemens, R. (Ed.). (2016). *The Voynich Manuscript*. New Haven: Yale University Press.
- Facsimile edition with essays
- Authoritative codicological description

Zandbergen, R. (2023). *Voynich Manuscript Studies*. http://www.voynich.nu/
- Comprehensive online resource
- Transcription data and analysis

### Paleographic Analysis

Davis, L. F. (2020). "How Many Glyphs and How Many Scribes? Digital Paleography and the Voynich Manuscript." *Manuscript Studies*, 5(1), 162-178.
- Definitive paleographic assessment
- Source of "nothing in history to compare it to" quote

Bowern, C. (2020). "Voynich Manuscript: Computational Methods." *Digital Scholarship in the Humanities*.
- Statistical analysis of script patterns

### Previous Decipherment Attempts

Bax, S. (2014). "A proposed partial decoding of the Voynich script." Self-published.
- Identified ~10 plant names
- Sound methodological approach, limited scope

Cheshire, G. (2019). "The Language and Writing System of MS408 (Voynich) Explained." *Romance Studies*, 37(2), 141-173.
- "Proto-Romance" hypothesis
- Rejected by linguistic community

Rugg, G. (2004). "An elegant hoax? A possible solution to the Voynich manuscript." *Cryptologia*, 28(1), 31-46.
- Cardan grille demonstration
- Proves hoax possibility, not actuality

### Comparison Corpora

- Roman cookbook
- Baseline comparison corpus

**Liber de Coquina** (14th c.). Neapolitan/French recipe collection.

**Antidotarium Nicolai** (12th c.). Salernitan pharmaceutical formulary.
- Medieval pharmaceutical structure reference

- Italian pharmaceutical context
