## ZFD Parsing Method Applied

**Operators (word-initial):** qo->ko, ch->h, sh->sh, da->da, ok->ost, ot->otr, q->k, o->o, d->d
**Gallows (medial):** k->st, t->tr, f->pr, p->pl
**Bench gallows:** cth->htr, ckh->hst, cph->hpl, cfh->hpr
**Suffixes (word-final, longest first):** aiin->ain, edy->edi, eey->ei, y->i, ol, ar, or, al, am, om, n, l, r, m
**Vowels:** o->o, a->a, e->e, i->i

Word-initial gallows (k, t, f, p appearing as the first character of a word) are expanded following the same rules as medial gallows, consistent with f88r precedent (e.g., L29: "kor" -> "stor").
